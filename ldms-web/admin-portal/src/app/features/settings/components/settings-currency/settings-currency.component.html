Functional currency (your books)

This is the currency your organisation uses for internal records — inventory unit prices, purchase requisitions, purchase orders, and financial reports. In Zimbabwe many companies keep their books in USD even when paying suppliers in ZWG.

Transaction currency

When you buy or sell in another currency (e.g. ZWG), the document stores the amount in that currency. The system converts to your functional currency using the rate locked at transaction time.

history

Rate changes never rewrite history

Updating an exchange rate (e.g. USD/ZWG from 26.8 to 28.0) applies only to new transactions. Invoices, payments, and GRV-linked bills already recorded keep their original converted amounts.

payments

Payment settlement & exchange gain/loss (IFRS 21)

All amounts are recorded in the organisation functional currency using the spot rate on the transaction date. On payment, the settlement-date rate is applied and realized exchange gain or loss is stored for audit.
